## What is an LLM in a business context?

A **Large Language Model (LLM)** is an AI system for natural language — it can read documents, answer questions, summarize content, and support decisions when connected to the right data.

Unlike legacy rule-based chatbots, LLMs handle context and flexible conversations much better.

## Common use cases

- **Customer support:** product questions and how-to guidance
- **Internal search:** policies, procedures, technical docs
- **Drafting & summarization:** meetings, emails, reports
- **Text analysis:** ticket classification and information extraction

## Deployment considerations

LLMs do **not** fully replace people. Businesses need:

1. **Private data (RAG)** — ground the model on internal documents
2. **Output control** — especially in healthcare and pharmacy
3. **Security** — clear policies before sending sensitive data externally
4. **Measurement** — accuracy, response time, satisfaction

## LLMs at KIT Technology

We integrate LLMs into **Novixa AI Assistant** and internal workflows: engineering assistance, document summarization, and pharmacy-facing assistants — always with content review and scoped data access.

## Conclusion

LLMs are powerful when placed correctly: **less repetitive work, faster lookup, better customer experience** — but they require the right architecture, data, and operating model.
