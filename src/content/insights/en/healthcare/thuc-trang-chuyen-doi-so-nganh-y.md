## The big picture

Healthcare and pharmacy in Vietnam are moving toward **digitized operations**: electronic records, prescribing workflows, supply chain visibility, and stronger regulatory reporting.

Progress is **uneven** — large hospitals lead while independent and chain pharmacies still rely on spreadsheets, ledgers, or disconnected tools.

## What has improved

- Cashless payments and e-invoicing
- Drug lookup, barcodes, basic inventory
- Mobile apps for staff and customers
- Real-time revenue reporting (in some chains)

## Persistent challenges

1. **Fragmented data** — sales, inventory, and CRM in silos
2. **GPP compliance** — hard to audit without a central system
3. **Batch and expiry control** — high risk when manual
4. **Limited integration standards** between systems
5. **Staff capacity** — pharmacists stuck at the counter instead of consulting

## A practical path for pharmacies

Effective transformation does not start with “the most expensive software”. It starts with:

- **One core platform** — POS, inventory, reporting, CRM on shared data
- **Standardized processes** — receiving, stocktake, returns, promotions
- **Phased expansion** — branches, customer app, AI assistance
